Frequently Asked Questions

A typing speed test measures how quickly you can type words on a keyboard. It calculates your speed in words per minute (WPM) and often also tracks your accuracy by counting errors.

Your typing speed is calculated by counting the number of words you type correctly in one minute. A "word" is typically considered to be five characters, including spaces.

A good typing speed depends on experience:
Beginner: 20–30 WPM
Average: 40–50 WPM
Advanced: 60–80 WPM
Professional: 90+ WPM
Most people aim for at least 40 WPM to be comfortable in everyday typing tasks.

Both speed and accuracy are essential. A high speed with low accuracy means you may need to correct mistakes frequently, reducing overall productivity. Most typing tests display both speed and accuracy scores.

Regular practice is the key to improving typing speed. Using typing software or games, focusing on correct finger placement, and avoiding looking at the keyboard can help improve both speed and accuracy over time.

WPM stands for Words Per Minute, while CPM stands for Characters Per Minute. CPM counts each keystroke, including spaces, while WPM groups characters into word units. Some tests use CPM, but WPM is more commonly used.

Different typing tests use different word lists and lengths, which can impact your speed and accuracy. Some tests also penalize errors more heavily, affecting your overall score.

Practicing for about 10-15 minutes each day is usually enough for steady improvement. Consistency is more important than long practice sessions, so regular, short practice works best.
